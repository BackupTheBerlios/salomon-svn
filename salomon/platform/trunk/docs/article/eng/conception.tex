\section{Conception of the \emph{Salomon} platform}

The proposed architecture of the Salomon platform
is a reply to some limitations of \emph{VINLEN}. The
process of gathering the knowledge from data is
usually very complex – some algorithms require
large amount of computations, on considerably
large amount of given data.
This is why the efficiency problems appear and
thus, during the designing  of the architecture of the
system, its scalability was also one of more important
aspects. We managed to attain it, thanks
to breaking the entire computational process into
isolated \emph{tasks} and it created the possibility of dispersed
calculations.

Main assumptions of the platform are:

\begin{description}
    \item[Component-based design.]
    \emph{VINLEN} is a complete product, delivered together with different 
    built-in algorithms. 

    \emph{Salomon} is an open
platform which is supposed to provide a basis
for other projects employing the Machine Learning
idea in the process of discovering the knowledge
in data. Hence, the assumption, that platform
– main module of the system – is as
simple as possible. Providing the user-friendly
environment was brought down to setting it for the realization
of the functionality contained in components
having the form of so-called plug-ins.

    \item[Open platform.] The user has full freedom
in adding new components (plug-ins) to the
system.

    \item[Independence from the runtime environment.]
		Thanks to using portable tools, for example \emph{Java}, the system can be
		started in any environment having the appropriate virtual machine.

    \item[Possibility of calculations distribution.]
		Thanks to component-based design and right definition of tasks, the system is
		enables work in the distributed, heterogeneous network environment.

    \item[Simplicity of using.]
    An easiness of using is one of bases of the \emph{VINLEN} system. \emph{Salomon}
    aims also in this respect achieve equal easiness and what is more: to outrun
    his predecessor. One of most expected facilitation is the replacement of the
    language of defining ''scouts'' with a graphical interface in the form of
    the graph depicting the knowledge flow.
\end{description}

System \emph{Salomon} consists of two components. First from them is a platform.
The second component is a set of plug-ins, providing the logic needed for 
configuring, executing and showing the results of execution. The platform
consists of two elements: the engine and the data storage. The former one is
responsible for managing and executing tasks and latter one stores the data and
knowledge. The possibility of elastic definition of  the course of the
processing is one of the essential advantages of the system. It is realized in
the form of so-called \emph{tasks}. An extraction of the knowledge is a process
which can be divided in stages – in every stage the knowledge is being subjected
to sure processing (e.g. attributes are discretized, the rules are created, the
knowledge is tested). Since every stage is creating a separate integrity and 
some of the stages are independent from each other,  they can be processed
simultaneously.

A task is a basic individual representing calculations.

It is defined as:
$$
z=(a, p, in, out)
$$
where:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $a$ is an algorithm, passed to the system in the form of a component –
    plug-in,

    \item $p$ represents parameters of the algorithm,
    \item $in$ and $out$ are the input and output information of the algorithm.
\end{itemize}

Input and output can have the form of data
(in the case of algorithms, which accomplishes
selection of data), of knowledge (e.g. generated
rules) or of both data and the knowledge
(e.g. rules and exceptions for them).

We expect that the exit of one task can become an access to certain number of
consequent tasks. A task can be performed only if all the dependent  asks have
been finished. Combined objectives can be introduced in the form of a directed 
graph. Such a graph with a marked initial task is creating the program.
Additionally, every edge can have an assigned condition, which is required in
order to hand over the guidance along  this edge. This way, the defined program
can easily be introduced the user in a graphical form. In the system it is
possible to define agents which will be reacting to changes in data and/or of
the knowledge (e.g. to adding a new record to training data or generating the 
knowledge by a different agent).
